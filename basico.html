How to change light saber colors

Description

In the lobby you can press Edit to change your saber to other sabers that you own. Some styles have different forms which you can customize in the top right. On the bottom right you can look at a basic overview on the stats of the lightsaber/form. Saber Stats Clicking on the color tab will allow you to change the color of your lightsaber. You can enter a custom lightsaber color with a hex code.

How to use Force Abilities

Description

Gain power focus by staring at an opponent for 3 seconds until you see smoke. The power focus you gain while looking at an opponent decays at the same rate that it increases. Having power focus will allow you to use different powers. You can bind powers in the top left or use them by switching them with X and pressing F to use them. Saber throw is unique in that you just press C to use it. Current powers include: Push, pull, grip, lightning, speed, sense, throw, and jump. Some notable power interactions include: You can use powers on opponents with less than 20 power points without needing power focus. Using pull/push on an enemy that is running will do a super push which will push them farther and knock them over. Using pull/push while being choked will do a super push on the choker even if you don’t have power focus. Lightning stops people from attacking if you have power focus. Getting hit without perfect blocking will result in losing 0.25 seconds of power focus.

Movement

Description

Movement is the fundamental core of any game. It is achieved by using the joystick on mobile or WASD on PC.Saber Showdown at its core has the same movement, with a few quirks. Pressing S automatically slows down a player considerably, making retreating using A and S significantly faster and safer. Pressing the spacebar or jump button will allow a player to do a normal jump, while holding jump allows a player to force jump, gaining in height while consuming force points. Jumping backwards prevents force jumping. The maximum speed of a force jump is equal to the speed the player was at the moment they hit the jump button. It is possible to start accelerating in other directions in the middle of a force jump. Hitting ctrl or the crouch button makes the user crouch, preventing them from being slapped, slowing down their movement speed, and preventing stamina regeneration. Hitting crouch and a movement direction right after landing allows the user to do a roll in a direction of their choosing, moving rapidly and escaping potential harm for a brief period. Holding down shift on PC, or toggling walk on mobile allows the user to walk, regenerating stamina, lessens the effects of force pull and force push, lowering movement speed, and giving a 20% attack buff.

Feint

Description

How to Feint: Press your attack button (M1/R1) immediately followed by or simultaneously with your ignite button (usually X or Q) to interrupt the swing animation. Purpose: Feinting forces opponents to react to a fake swing, causing them to miss their perfect block timing. This leaves them vulnerable to a follow-up attack. Usage: Highly effective against opponents who rely on consistent perfect blocking, as it disrupts their timing and forces them to re-evaluate their defense

Perfect Block

Description

**Perfect Blocking – Key Points** * **What it is:** Move your crosshair to the direction of the incoming swing (e.g., WA = top left). * **Why it matters:** A successful perfect block = **0 BP loss**, essential for winning duels. **Core Skills** * Zone knowledge * Fast reactions * Prediction **Tips** * **Crosshair placement:** Keep it high (around W zone) for faster reaction to common swings (W, WD, WA). * **Overlaps:** Learn positions that block multiple directions (e.g., SD/D or SA/A). * **Distance:** Stay a bit further away → easier reactions and less extreme mouse movement. **Practice Methods** * **Repetition:** Have someone repeat the same swing until you learn the correct zone. * **Delayed reaction:** Focus on identifying the swing first, then moving correctly → speed improves over time.

Swing Block

Description

Swing blocking is one of the most fundamental parts to being good at dueling in MB2. Swing blocking allows you to attack your opponent whilst simultaneously blocking, meaning that you cannot be slapped or disarmed. You are also able to regenerate block points while swing blocking. To swing block, simply tap left click to attack, and then hold down right click straight after. 7. Perfect blocking. Perfect blocking is very important, and also one of the hardest to get consistent. Being good at perfect blocking requires good knowledge of the zones, quick reactions and an element of prediction. To perfect block, you simply move your mouse to the designated area the swing is coming from - if your opponent swings WA then you look to their top left, for example. Successfully perfect blocking results in you losing no BP from an opponents swing, so they are crucial to surviving duels. Advice and good habit: Crosshair placement: Always keep your crosshair high above the opponents head around the area of the ‘W’ zone. This allows quick reaction to the most common (W, WD, WA) swings. Overlaps: Try to figure out any areas of overlap, for example there are places you can look that will block both SD and D swings/SA and A swings in one location. Distance: Distance affects the area of the zones, because they are relative to the model. This means that the closer you are, the more extreme your mouse movements need to be. Also, keeping your distance gives you a longer chance to react and makes PBing easier.
