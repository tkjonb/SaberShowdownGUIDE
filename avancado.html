ADVANCED SS DEFINITIVE GUIDE

Counter Swing

Description

Counters are done by anticipating your opponents swing, perfect blocking them, and then attacking back right as they hit you, this results in a very quick swing which hits your opponent in return. The counter swing window lasts 0.1 seconds after getting hit. If you perfect block a slow swing that’s the first swing of the enemy’s combo, you can do a maximum of 2 swings or however many swings you have left in your combo. If you perfect block in the middle of your opponents combo, you can combo regularly.

Combo Reset/Extended

Description

Swing Reset: Consists of stopping the attack on the last hit of the combo (usually the second or third) and waiting for the saber to fully return to the blocking position before starting a new combo. Slap Reset: Involves using the slap command shortly after the last hit of the combo. For greater effectiveness, it is recommended to perform the slap at the exact moment the hit strikes the opponent, followed by a small pause before starting to attack again.

Flick Yaw

Description

Yawing for fast attack speed. This refers to a yawwing where the attack starting point is pulled towards the opponent in an instant.[4] At first glance, it appears to be rotating randomly, but in reality, the attack start point is precisely pointed at the enemy by dragging. With one basic lightsaber, 1 normal swing + 3 combo swings were cast with Flick Yawing. Flick Yawing can also be mixed with a half swing . It is a basic lightsaber style, 1 normal attack + 3 half swings , cast with Flick Yawing. It is a basic lightsaber style, 1 normal attack + 3 half swings , cast with Flick Yawing. It is usually very difficult to perfectly defend against a flick yaw , but there are those who can perfectly defend by understanding the direction of rotation and the opponent's attack pattern . One thing to be careful of is that when using this technique, the distance from the opponent must be close. This is because the angle at which you need to turn with the flick yaw changes as the distance from the opponent changes, but if the distance from the opponent exceeds a certain level, the error becomes too large and the flick yaw does not unfold properly. There is also a technique that increases the speed of Flick Yawing to the maximum.

Delay Swing

Description

Half swinging is fairly important in building ACM and allows for somewhat more defensive play, especially if combined with Shadow swinging (see later). To half swing, swing once then wait until your saber reaches its full animation, just before the animation is about to finish, attack in the same, or a different, direction. You should be able to feel the timing, and you will see how the swing is performed quicker. Practicing: Use red at first to observe half swinging, because it is far more noticeable with red than it is yellow. To practice half swing with yellow, just stand still and practice the timing on the air, you should aim to be able to tap the timing and swing block each hit (it’s far easier to swing block than combos). The timing is the same in air versus hitting your opponent so there is no need to use dummies etc.

Dex Swing

Description

This is a technique that allows the combo to continue longer than the maximum number of times by using the skip function of the slash preparation phase of the slap , and requires high skill level. How to do it (based on form 1): As soon as the third swing hits the opponent, a slap is triggered and the combo count is reset. Press the attack button to continue the attack with a normal swing (Full Swing) .[3] At this time, the slap must be activated before the end of the slash phase . If you use slap later than this, a combo swing will be activated or the attack will be cut off, making it impossible to continue the attack.
